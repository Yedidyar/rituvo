# Canonical Terminology

Use these terms consistently in code, product copy, analytics, and documentation.

## Habit

A repeated behavior the user wants to perform consistently.

Examples:

- Study for 20 minutes
- Walk every morning
- Practice guitar

## Goal

A broader desired outcome that may contain one or more habits or milestones.

Goals are not required for the first demo.

## Commitment

A specific promise to perform a habit under defined conditions.

A commitment should include a completion rule and schedule.

## Commitment period

The time window in which one expected action should occur.

Examples:

- Today
- Monday morning
- This week

## Check-in

The user's recorded outcome for a commitment period.

## Completion

A check-in that satisfies the defined minimum completion rule.

## Miss

A commitment period that ended without meeting the completion rule.

## Recovery

The next constructive action after a miss.

Recovery is a first-class product concept.

## Accountability partner

A person who can see and respond to the user's commitment activity according to the relationship permissions.

## Accountability relationship

The connection between two users that defines shared visibility and allowed interactions.

## Encouragement

A lightweight supportive response intended to help action.

## Acknowledgment

A response to a completed check-in.

## Evidence

Optional material that supports a check-in, such as a note, duration, quantity, or photo.

## Streak

The number of consecutive commitment periods completed under the selected streak rule.

Streak rules must be explicit.

## Reliability

A broader measure of fulfilled commitments over time.

Do not treat reliability as identical to a streak.

## Challenge

A shared, time-limited commitment experience.

## Reward

A benefit unlocked by progress, reliability, participation, or challenge completion.

## Today view

The primary surface showing current commitments and the next useful action.

## Avoided terms

Avoid using the following as substitutes for core domain entities:

- Task, when the object is actually a commitment
- Post, when the object is actually a check-in
- Friend, when the relationship is specifically accountability
- Score, without naming what it measures
