# Users and Jobs to Be Done

## Primary user

A person who wants to build or maintain a habit but struggles with consistency, procrastination, or acting alone.

Typical examples:

- A student trying to study regularly
- A person trying to exercise
- A creator trying to publish consistently
- A professional trying to complete focused work
- A person trying to practice a skill
- Friends building a shared routine

## Secondary user

An accountability partner who helps another person stay committed while also receiving accountability.

The same person may be both a primary and secondary user.

## Future user groups

Potential later audiences include:

- Coaches
- Mentors
- Small communities
- Schools
- Teams
- Wellness programs
- Brands offering benefits
- Challenge organizers

These are not required for the first demo.

## Core jobs to be done

### When I decide to improve something

Help me turn a vague intention into a specific commitment.

### When motivation is low

Make it easier to act because another person is involved.

### When I complete the habit

Let me record progress quickly and feel that it mattered.

### When I miss a commitment

Help me recover without pretending nothing happened or making me quit.

### When I support someone else

Show me when and how I can be useful.

### When I look back

Help me understand whether I am becoming more consistent.

## User needs

Users need:

- Clarity
- Low-friction check-ins
- Emotional safety
- Useful social pressure
- Visible progress
- Flexible recovery
- Control over privacy
- Confidence that the system reflects reality

## Demo persona

For the first demo, use a simple representative persona:

**Name:** Maya  
**Goal:** Complete a 20-minute focused study session each weekday  
**Accountability partner:** Alex  
**Main difficulty:** Starting on time and recovering after skipped days  
**Desired outcome:** Build a reliable study routine without relying on motivation alone

The names and habit may be replaced in the UI, but the product behavior should remain equivalent.
